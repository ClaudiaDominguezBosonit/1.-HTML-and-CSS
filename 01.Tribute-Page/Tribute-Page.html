John Laurens

American Revolutionary War officer

The life of John Laurens

28th October 1754

John Laurens is born

4th August 1777

John applied to join George Washington's staff as an aide-de-camp.

11th September 1777

Battle of Brandywine

4th October 1777

Battle of Germantown

6th October 1777

Laurens is officially Washington's official aide joining Alexander Hamilton and Richard Meade.

28th June 1778

Battle of Monmouth

3rd May 1779

Battle of Coosawhatchie

July 1779

Congress approves of Lauren's black regiment plan and let him recruit 3,000 slaves from South Carolina and Georgia. John goes to Charleston and tells John Rutledge and the Privy Council about his plan. They could've either arm slaves and then free them or surrender. South Carolina government -made up os mostly slave owners- said no and even asked Laurens to carry out surrender terms - which John refuses.

16th September 1779

Siege of Savannah: John led a light infantry. Many of his men retreated so he threw his sword on the ground.

21st October 1779

Congress elect him secretary to Benjamin Franklin, but he formally declined the appointment because he considered being an officer meant more than being a statesman.

28th September 1781

Battle of Yorktown

27th August 1782

Laurens proceeded to Chehaw Neck, unaware that the British had already boarding their ships. When the Americans charged, the British opened fire. Laurens feel from his horse mortally wounded.

"Whereas other men considered property the basis of liberty, Laurens believed liberty that rested on the sweat of slaves was not deserving of the name. To that extent, at least, his beliefs make him our contemporary, a man worthy of more attention than the footnote he has been in most accounts of the American Revolution."

-- Gregory D. Massey, a history professor at Freed-Hardeman University and author of a Laurens biography
